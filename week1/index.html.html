How to Improve UX by Understanding Users

  • Be There - Be there to help users through their decision journey, and deepen their loyalty.
  • Be Useful - Don't make users hunt for what they want. Provide them with what they came for as quickly as possible. Only 9% of users will stay if it doesn't satisfy their needs.
  • Be Quick - You need to reduce the users cognitive load. Not just fast loading, functionality has to be there as well. Doing five steps will frustrate you a bit, doing zero steps will make you happy without even thinking about it.
  • Reachability (How Users handle their devices) Matters
  • Speed Matters (Network)- Speed and Loading times of the app matters. People are more likely to stay when things load fast. One of the biggest things that affects speed are network speed. Not everybody has the latest and greatest internet speed.
    • Reduce images used throughout the apps/sites
    • SVG/Webfonts are lower bandwidth
    • Optimize and Minify CSS and JS files
    • GZIP files to eliminate unnecessary data

Designing Intuitive and Mobile Friendly Forms

Make your forms simple, so people can use it easily. Make them think less.
  • Use float labels
  • Make the inputs large enough
  • Make checkboxes larger
  • Use single column layout
  • Get rid of irrelevant fields, only ask for information you need
  • If the form requires more information, break it down into sections, make use of steps or pagination. Make sure to include a process indicator.
  • Enable Autocomplete/autofill on your forms.
  • Passwords matter - Instead of hiding passwords by default, show them, but always allow the users to hide them easily if they need to
  • Numbers matter
    • build credit card inputs in such a way that its easy for the users to add their information. Make it easy to read as they type in the info.
    • When taking numbers, ensure the number keypad is used on mobile, default keyboards make it cumbersome to enter numbers only.
